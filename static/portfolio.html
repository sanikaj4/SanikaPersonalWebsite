AI Gender and Financial Literacy Outcomes

UX Research | Behavioral Analysis | R Programming

As part of CMU's 67-368 "UX Research for Product Managers" course, I conducted a study examining how the perceived gender of AI agents (male vs. female) affects user performance on financial literacy tasks. Using data collected via Prolific and analyzed in R, I connected behavioral insights with interface design implications. The final presentation showcased research methodology, statistical analysis, and user experience considerations.
